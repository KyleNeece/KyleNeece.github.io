**Introduction Section**
- "Soon after Japan’s surrender in August 1945, US forces occupied the Korean Peninsula south of the 38th parallel, while Soviet forces occupied the northern part of the peninsula. The United States established a military government in its occupation zone—the United States Army Military Government in Korea (USAMGIK, 1945–1948)—and sought to set up there a US-friendly regime." pp2 of pdf
- American missionaries played key in the years leading up to establishment of the Republic of Korea south of the 38th parallel
- Fisher (we know him!) traveled to Korea as a educational missionary and professor 1919-1934
	- Goal was to introduce John Deweys ideas on democracy, education and tried to reinterpret goals of mission education in colonial Korea
- Returned to US in 1934 worked government and served as an officer for CIA 
- "The source will deal with both Fisher’s ideas on democracy during the colonial period, and his post-1945 understanding of democracy. Further, this paper will delineate the contours of the democratic projects of the United States in South Korea by way of Fisher’s work for US public relations on democratic education in post-colonial South Korea." pp4 of the Pdf
	- What to look for 

**"Fishers activities..." section**
- Arrived in Korea in 1919
- Fisher brought the ideas of nationalism, communism, socialism and atheism. Fisher sought to apply the new methodology of democratic education to Korea and tackles the new trends among the young Korean body who were leaning to Wilsonian and Leninist ideologies 
- Fisher left Korea on good terms with Japan colonial authorities (1928?)
	- After however, Fisher believed Japan was becoming a totalitarian state 
	- “the police and secret detectives were active day and night trying to find those who might not be loyal or who might in some way try to sabotage the great enterprise on which the Empire was embarking” pp6 of Pdf

**"The Educator and his democracy education" section**
- Fishers Democracy and Mission education in Korea based upon his dissertation that was accepted by Columbia University in 1927
- Fisher wanted to integrate mission education in Korea with democracy under the marked influence of John Dewey and William H. Kilpatrick 
- Fisher - how did he relate mission education to democracy (pp8 of the Pdf)
	1. Draw up the criteria of democratic education 
	2. Reconstruct the aims of mission education 
	3. Study the relationship between mission education and government control of education 
	4. Study the relationship between mission education and the problems in the daily lives of the Korean people
- Fisher stated democracy has been expanded and deepened to apply to all institutions be it political, social, economic, educational and religious 
- Continues to say that the aim of education should be to find out more about life and to increase and enrich, so we can enable human beings to live more satisfying lives to satisfy themselves and others
- Fisher stated that 
	- “The aim of mission education in Korea is for missionaries to work with Koreans in their various life activities, for the purpose of bringing both themselves and the Koreans to a better understanding of life, and to a better control of the forces which make for the fullest and richest living” pp9 of the Pdf
- Fishers democracy definition was expressed in adjective form rather then noun 
- Counsel is better than direction and is the best policy thus there will be less demand for what might be classified as all-round missionaries 
- Fishers ideas were acceptable to some Korean intellectuals and missionaries 
- "Mission education must be more scientific, human and more educational" pp10 of the Pdf
- Fisher essentially passed torch to Horace Underwood to be a educational missionary 

**"Fishers activities during US Military Occupation" section**
- Korean political system had impact on Fishers efforts at democracy education
	- "The first was that Korea’s current political parties were not parties in the modern sense but more manifestations of clientele politics, for they were merely groups “gathered around a few leaders, and that their purpose is to gain possession of the government in order to distribute jobs and special favors to their constituents" pp14 of the Pdf
	- "The second key factor was that “the best organized and most active body in politics” was the communist party, which was heavily funded by the Soviets in northern Korea" pp14 of the Pdf 
- Due to Korea attempting to partner with Russia, US state department implemented that 
	- “the Commander of United States Forces in Korea shall take steps to institute a broad program of constructive economic and educational reforms for Southern Korea looking toward the creation of conditions favorable to the development of a strong and lasting democratic system in Korea” pp17 of the Pdf

**"Fishers ideas...." section**
- Democracy is a “a government in which the supreme power is vested in the people and exercised by them directly or indirectly through a system of representation usually involving periodically held free elections. ”20 Democracy usually requires “pluralism and the recognition that we need to find fair terms of living together as free, equal, but also irreducibly diverse citizens” pp18 of the Pdf
- Democracy and totalitarianism are antitheses to one another (p19 of the Pdf)
	- Totalitarianism was a reference to the USSR at the time 
- Fisher’s focus was on the value and liberty of the individual, as well as diverse aspects of a democratic society, such as family, school, entrepreneurship, employment, media, the military, courts, and social institutions; he did not deal with socio-economic issues such as land tenure, the unequal distribution of property, and social justice. pp21 of the reading 
- Fisher had good credit with President Rhee and left on good terms with him 
- Fisher and Rhee's relationship were strained due to organizational issues with Koreans from the northwest 
- Rhee vs Fisher 
	- Fisher supported more lenient and democratic methods while President Rhee did not. Also, Fisher supported some Korean elites who criticized Rhee on the basis of procedural democracy, and these elites happened to be northerners. pp24 of the Pdf

**Conclusion Section**
- Fisher tried to understand the new social and intellectual trends and apply them to his teachings and writtings
- Fisher failed to question the relationship between democracy and post-colonial issues such as national division and pro Japan collaboration 
- Fishers case shows the US government at least with good faith tried to implant democracy into the newly liberated Korea 
	- Fishers view of it however last limited to a American experience 
		- This in turn made an attempt to forge a democracy variety that was between conservative interpretation of liberal democracy and a democracy of oppressive Cold War authoritarian regime
