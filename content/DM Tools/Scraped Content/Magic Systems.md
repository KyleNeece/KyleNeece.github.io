**Cause, effect and the unmoved mover:** the entire cosmos is fundamentally unified by the principle of cause and effect. In order to rely on his logical theory of cause and effect at work in the perceptible world, Aristotle held that there must also be an immortal, unchanging being, ultimately responsible for all wholeness and orderliness on earth and in the cosmos (Aristotle Metaphysics, book 12)
