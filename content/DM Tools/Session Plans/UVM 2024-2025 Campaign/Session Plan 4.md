- Reach a bridge, and a huge troll blocks the front of the bridge. He demands payment, and always says “you pay! 200 pay!”

The miscellaneous of land 
- First encounter, the lonely door 
	- The Lonely Door. This door isn't trapped. It's not locked. It asks a riddle as the players approach. When they answer, it accepts the answer and asks another riddle. And then another... It will keep this up forever.
	- Most players are conditioned to assume that a riddle must be solved before they can progress. That's not true for this door - it just wants the characters to hang out with it, and has learnt that this is the best way to do that
- Second Encounter, the formidable foe 
	- The party comes across a foe way above their caliber. Basically, a "it's not worth the risk to fight it". But, there's a silver lining: the creature doesn't understand the difference between saying an action and performing that action. It also doesn't fully understand the rules of those actions, and is basically operating mostly on children's play pretend rules (where kids start being like "no I have a force field" or "no actually fireballs don't do that to me"). So the party gets into basically a match of this play pretend with it, and has to try and argue for their own effects with more logic and "inavoidability" than the creature does. If your party don't like rp (since this puzzle only works if they don't paraphrase), it may not work for them
- Third Encounter, the sleeping Dragon
	- An Elderly Green Dragon lazing on the final part of the Miscellaneous of Land. It collects a toll from people who pass by. And the local wood elves keep it fed as it’s getting too old to hunt for itself.
	- Between the constant influx of food and gold it’s content to sleep most of the time and not be actively evil. The Dragon sleeps so often the party can try to risk sneaking past it over the bridge or attempting something else. They alternatively could pay the exorbitant cost of the tolls by offering it gold, magic loot or conducting a lengthy hunt to appease it with a feast of food
	- If they want, they could try and fight. Which would upset not only the Dragon but the shadow circle as well, who have been taking care of it for years
