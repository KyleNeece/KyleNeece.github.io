Main Story Plot 
- Peacekeepers of the multiverse 
- Located at Sigil, recent new defense force that recruits talented individuals to protect the multiverse 
- Different heats of missions (E rank - S rank) 

Welcome to [[Sigil]], aka the center of the multiverse. Sigil is the main hub for interplanar travel. You’ll meet all kinds of exotic creatures and experience all kinds of exotic cultures, and travel to many different planes, exploring the cosmology of the dnd (and potentially other) multiverse. Let’s take a look at the brief history of the situation: When Lady of Pain, Sigil’s ruler and founder, went corrupt and threatened the safety of the multiverse, a group of 6 adventurers took swift action to rebel and put her down for good. A battle between heroes and tyrants Will, with heavy losses on each side. After the dust settled, only one man survived… Will Felwinter, a Human Eldritch Knight Fighter who was a part of the powerful but now endangered Felwinter bloodline. To ensure the safety of the multiverse, Will Felwinter established an academy for young aspiring adventurers located on Sigil, with its goal being to prepare them for the worst threats possible. 2 childhood friends known as the The Twin Prodigies; Jackson Felwinter the Human Magus; and Azriel Darkstar the Nephelm Runekeeper, stood at the top of the class, holding a level of power that has never been seen before. Despite their promising ability to potentially bring peace to all of the multiverse, tragedy occurred, as the invasion of Tartarus, Enslaver of Worlds almost destroyed the planet. The war lost great assets that day, with Will Felwinter and Azriel Darkstar perishing in the process, with Jackson Felwinter landing a lucky ace in the hole blow against Tartarus, ending the invasion. Jackson Felwinter, along with the rest of his friends, established a new force called the Peacekeepers, an interplanar covert force that works in secret behind the scenes of the cosmos to eliminate both big and small threats to ensure the safety of the multiverse. Ever since this program was created, millions of adventurers and even common folk rushed to sign up for the test in hopes of achieving their lifelong dream of becoming a peacekeeper. Our story begins with eight individuals, with enormous potential, enrolling and beginning their trials to become a Peacekeeper so they can set out on adventure and uncover the mysteries of the reality they live in….

###### **Combat** Encounter: Defense of the Fort
- **Combat** Encounter: The Siege of Blackstone Keep
- **Objective**: Defend the Keep from an invading army and protect the civilians inside.
- **Background**: The Keep is a strategic fortress situated on a hill overlooking a vital trade route. The keep is under threat from a powerful enemy force led by a ruthless warlord. The soldiers are tasked with defending the keep and ensuring the safety of the townsfolk seeking refuge within its walls.

**Setting**
- **The Keep:** 
	- **Location:** A fortress on a hill, surrounded by thick stone walls with watchtowers at each corner. The keep has a main gate and a smaller sally port.
	- **Layout:** The keep consists of an outer courtyard, inner courtyard, barracks, armory, and the main keep where the civilians are sheltered. There are also underground tunnels for emergency escapes.
	- **Defenses:** Ballistae, boiling oil cauldrons, archers' slits, and a drawbridge.

**Encounter Breakdown**
- Number of troops gained for the soldiers 
	- 1d6 Bandits 
	- 1d10 Guards

**1. The Enemy Approaches**
- **Description:** As dawn breaks, scouts report the sighting of the enemy army advancing towards Blackstone Keep. The soldiers must quickly prepare the defenses and rally the defenders.
- **Challenges:**
	- **Organizing Defenses:** Assigning roles to the keep’s defenders, positioning archers, and manning the ballistae. (Wisdom (Insight) or Intelligence (Investigation) checks to optimize defenses)
	- **Rallying the Troops:** Inspiring the defenders to stand firm against the approaching threat. (Charisma (Persuasion) or Charisma (Intimidation) checks)
**2. The Initial Assault**
- **Description:** The enemy launches a full-scale assault on the main gate, using battering rams, siege towers, and ladders. Waves of enemy soldiers attempt to breach the walls.
- **Challenges:**
	- **Holding the Gate:** Defending the main gate from being broken down. (Combat encounter with enemy soldiers and battering ram operators)
	- **Repelling Siege Towers:** Destroying or repelling the siege towers before they reach the walls. (Dexterity (Ranged Attack) to use ballistae, Strength (Athletics) to push ladders off the walls)
	- **Archery Defense:** Using archers to pick off enemy soldiers climbing the walls. (Dexterity (Ranged Attack) checks)
**3. The Breach**
- **Description:** Despite the defenders’ best efforts, a section of the wall is breached. The enemy pours into the outer courtyard, and the soldiers must fight to contain the breach and prevent the enemy from reaching the inner courtyard.
- **Challenges:**
	- **Contain the Breach:** Engaging in close-quarters combat to hold the breach and push back the invading forces. (Combat encounter with enemy infantry)
	- **Protecting the Civilians:** Ensuring the civilians are safely escorted to the inner keep. (Dexterity (Stealth) or Wisdom (Perception) to navigate safely, Charisma (Persuasion) to calm the civilians)
**4. The Warlord’s Challenge**
- **Description:** The enemy warlord, a fearsome and heavily armored fighter, enters the fray, challenging the defenders to single combat. Defeating the warlord could turn the tide of battle.
- **Challenges:**
	- **Single Combat:** One of the soldiers faces the warlord in a duel. (Combat encounter with the warlord, utilizing tactical combat skills and abilities)
	- **Supporting the Duel:** The other soldiers can provide support by dealing with any remaining enemy soldiers and preventing interference. (Combat encounter with remaining enemy forces)
**Additional Details (rough ideas)**
- **Enemy Forces:**
	- **Infantry:** Basic enemy soldiers, lightly armored with swords and shields.
	- **Archers:** Enemy archers providing ranged support.
	- **Siege Equipment Operators:** Specialized units operating the battering rams and siege towers.
	- **Warlord:** The enemy leader, a formidable opponent with heavy armor and powerful melee attacks.
- **Environmental Factors:**
	- **Weather:** A storm begins to brew during the battle, making the terrain slippery and reducing visibility. (Dexterity (Acrobatics) checks to maintain footing, Wisdom (Perception) checks for visibility)
	- **Fire:** Fires break out in parts of the keep due to enemy attacks, creating hazards and potential choke points. (Dexterity (Acrobatics) or Strength (Athletics) checks to navigate through burning areas)

###### Exploration Encounter: The Hidden Path
- **Objective:** Navigate through a dense forest to find a hidden path leading to an ancient ruin.
- **Setup:**
	- The forest is thick with undergrowth, creating difficult terrain and reducing visibility.
	- There are natural hazards such as quicksand, poisonous plants, and dangerous wildlife.
- **Details:**
	- The PCs must use their skills (e.g., Survival, Perception, Nature) to navigate through the forest.
	- Clues and landmarks, such as ancient carvings on trees or animal trails, guide them toward the hidden path.
	- Random encounters with forest creatures (friendly or hostile) add to the challenge.
- **Challenges:**
	- The PCs may face environmental puzzles, such as crossing a ravine or deciphering ancient markings.
	- Weather conditions can change, making navigation more difficult (e.g., sudden rainstorm, fog).
	- Time is a factor, as they must find the path before nightfall or a looming threat catches up to them.
- **Success Condition:** The PCs locate the hidden path and reach the ancient ruin without becoming lost or succumbing to the forest's dangers.

###### Role-Playing Encounter: The Diplomacy Test

  

**Objective:** Negotiate peace between two warring factions in a tense diplomatic meeting.

  

**Setup:**

- The meeting takes place in a neutral location, such as a grand hall or a sacred grove.

- Representatives from both factions are present, each with their own demands and grievances.

  

**Details:**

- The PCs must use their social skills (e.g., Persuasion, Insight, Deception) to navigate the complex negotiations.

- Each faction has specific issues that need addressing, such as territorial disputes, resource sharing, and past offenses.

- The PCs can gather information beforehand about the factions to better understand their motivations and weaknesses.

  

**Challenges:**

- There are hidden agendas and potential saboteurs within the meeting who might try to disrupt the peace talks.

- The factions are deeply mistrustful of each other, and any misstep can lead to renewed hostilities.

- The PCs must balance being firm and fair, avoiding favoritism while pushing for a resolution.

  

**Success Condition:** The PCs successfully broker a peace agreement that satisfies both factions and prevents further conflict.

  

These encounters test the PCs' abilities in combat, exploration, and role-playing, ensuring a well-rounded trial for aspiring soldiers.

  

### Social Encounter: The Peace Negotiation

  

**Objective:** Negotiate a peace treaty between two warring factions.

  

**Background:** The kingdom has been torn apart by a prolonged conflict between two rival factions: the Mountain Clan, a group of rugged warriors who inhabit the mountainous regions, and the River Guild, a coalition of traders and townsfolk who live along the river valleys. The king has sent the soldiers to mediate peace talks and broker a treaty to end the hostilities.

  

**Setting:** The negotiations take place in a grand hall within a neutral castle, situated on an island in the middle of a serene lake. The hall is richly decorated, with banners from both factions hanging side by side. A large round table sits in the center of the room, surrounded by chairs for the negotiators.

  

### Encounter Breakdown

  

**1. The Arrival**

  

**Objective:** Negotiate a peace treaty between two warring factions.

  

**Background:** The kingdom has been torn apart by a prolonged conflict between two rival factions: the Mountain Clan and the River Guild. The king has sent the soldiers to mediate peace talks and broker a treaty to end the hostilities.

  

### Key Figures and Their Backgrounds

  

**1. Chief Thorn - Leader of the Mountain Clan**

  

**Background:** Chief Thorn is a seasoned warrior and the respected leader of the Mountain Clan, a group of rugged and fiercely independent people who inhabit the mountainous regions of the kingdom. Thorn is known for his bravery in battle and his deep sense of honor. He has led his people through many hardships, including harsh winters and territorial disputes. His primary concern is the safety and autonomy of his clan.

  

**What They Are Negotiating For:**

- **Autonomy:** Chief Thorn wants to ensure that the Mountain Clan can govern themselves without interference from the River Guild or the kingdom's central authority.

- **Resource Rights:** He seeks exclusive rights to the valuable minerals and resources found in the mountains.

- **Security Guarantees:** Thorn demands assurances that the River Guild will not encroach on their territory and that any past grievances will be addressed.

  

**2. Lady Rose - Head of the River Guild**

  

**Background:** Lady Rose is the eloquent and strategic leader of the River Guild, a coalition of traders, artisans, and townsfolk who live along the river valleys. She is a skilled diplomat and businesswoman, having expanded the Guild's influence and trade networks significantly. Rose values prosperity and stability for her people, and she believes that a peaceful resolution is the best way to achieve this.

  

**What They Are Negotiating For:**

- **Trade Routes:** Lady Rose seeks guaranteed access to trade routes through the mountains, which are vital for the Guild's commerce.

- **Water Rights:** She wants to secure the rights to the river's water for irrigation and transportation, ensuring the Guild's economic stability.

- **Reparations:** Rose demands reparations for the damages inflicted on the River Guild’s towns and trade routes during the conflict.

  

**3. Advisors and Seconds**

  

**Background:** Each leader is accompanied by trusted advisors and seconds who have their own perspectives and interests. These individuals can provide valuable insights or pose additional challenges during the negotiations.

  

**Mountain Clan Advisors:**

- **Garrick, the War Chief:** Thorn’s right-hand man, who is skeptical of peace and prefers a strong, defensive stance.

- **Mara, the Wise Woman:** An elder who advises Thorn on spiritual matters and the clan’s traditions, advocating for a harmonious resolution.

  

**River Guild Advisors:**

- **Lyle, the Merchant Prince:** A wealthy trader who supports Rose and seeks to expand the Guild’s influence.

- **Cora, the Master Artificer:** An expert in crafting and enchantments, who emphasizes the importance of maintaining the Guild’s technological edge.

  

### Detailed Negotiation Points

  

**Autonomy and Governance:**

- Chief Thorn wants explicit terms that guarantee the Mountain Clan's right to self-governance. He fears that without clear terms, the River Guild or the kingdom’s central authority might impose laws that undermine his people’s way of life.

- Lady Rose is willing to concede some autonomy but wants assurances that the Mountain Clan will abide by certain trade and defense agreements that benefit the whole kingdom.

  

**Resource Rights and Trade Routes:**

- The Mountain Clan controls several mines rich in precious minerals, which are vital to the River Guild’s artisans and traders. Thorn wants exclusive rights to these resources, while Rose argues for shared access to ensure economic prosperity.

- Rose proposes a compromise where the River Guild invests in the infrastructure of the Mountain Clan’s mining operations in exchange for a percentage of the output.

  

**Security and Territorial Integrity:**

- Thorn demands a clear demarcation of borders and a non-aggression pact to ensure his clan’s security.

- Rose wants similar guarantees for the River Guild’s territories and seeks a cooperative defense agreement against external threats, suggesting joint patrols along their shared borders.

  

**Water Rights and Environmental Concerns:**

- The river is a crucial resource for both factions. Rose needs assurances that the Mountain Clan’s activities won’t disrupt the river’s flow or pollute the water.

- Thorn is willing to negotiate water rights but wants a voice in any decisions affecting the river’s management to protect his clan’s fishing and agricultural activities.

  

**Reparations and Compensation:**

- Rose insists on reparations for the economic losses suffered by the River Guild during the conflict, proposing a series of payments or resource shipments from the Mountain Clan.

- Thorn counters by highlighting the damages his people have endured and suggests a mutual aid agreement where both sides support each other in rebuilding efforts.

  

**Challenges:**

- **First Impressions:** The soldiers must make a positive impression on both factions. (Charisma (Persuasion) or Charisma (Diplomacy) checks)

- **Gathering Information:** The soldiers can speak with the advisors and seconds to gather insights into each faction's true goals and grievances. (Wisdom (Insight) or Intelligence (Investigation) checks)

  

**2. The Opening Session**

  

**Description:** The negotiations begin with both factions presenting their initial demands and grievances. The atmosphere is tense, and both sides are wary of each other.

  

**Challenges:**

- **Mediation:** The soldiers must listen carefully and mediate between the factions, finding common ground and suggesting compromises. (Charisma (Persuasion) or Wisdom (Insight) checks)

- **Handling Interruptions:** Unexpected events, such as a heated argument or an outburst from one of the advisors, must be managed to keep the talks on track. (Charisma (Intimidation) or Wisdom (Insight) checks)

  

**3. The Private Meetings**

  

**Description:** During a break in the negotiations, the soldiers have the opportunity to meet privately with key figures from each faction. These meetings are crucial for understanding the deeper issues and hidden agendas.

  

**Challenges:**

- **Persuasion:** Convincing key figures to support the peace process and make necessary concessions. (Charisma (Persuasion) or Charisma (Deception) checks)

- **Gathering Leverage:** Discovering secrets or vulnerabilities that can be used to influence the negotiations. (Intelligence (Investigation) or Wisdom (Insight) checks)

  

**4. The Crisis**

  

**Description:** As the negotiations progress, an assassination attempt is made on one of the faction leaders. The soldiers must act quickly to prevent chaos and salvage the peace talks.

  

**Challenges:**

- **Preventing the Assassination:** Identifying and stopping the assassin before they can strike. (Perception, Investigation, or combat checks as needed)

  

- **Maintaining Order:** Keeping both factions calm and preventing the incident from derailing the negotiations. (Charisma (Intimidation) or Wisdom (Insight) checks)

- **Investigating the Incident:** Determining who is behind the assassination attempt and exposing their motives. (Intelligence (Investigation) or Wisdom (Insight) checks)

  

**5. The Final Agreement**

  

**Description:** With the crisis averted, the soldiers must bring both factions back to the table and finalize the peace treaty. This requires addressing the core issues and ensuring both sides feel their interests are protected.

  

**Challenges:**

- **Drafting the Treaty:** Crafting a fair and balanced agreement that addresses the needs and concerns of both factions. (Intelligence (History) or Charisma (Persuasion) checks)

- **Securing Signatures:** Convincing the leaders and their advisors to sign the treaty and commit to the peace process. (Charisma (Persuasion) or Charisma (Intimidation) checks)

- **Celebrating the Peace:** Organizing a celebratory feast to solidify the new alliance and foster goodwill between the factions. (Charisma (Performance) or Charisma (Persuasion) checks)
