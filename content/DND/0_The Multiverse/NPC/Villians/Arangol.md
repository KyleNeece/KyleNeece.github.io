##### Brief Summary 
Arangol is a powerful Illithid Lich of unknown origin and is one of the main overarching villains within the Felwinter Saga

##### Personality  
Arangol is a power hungry sadistic manipulator. He is described as an embodiment of evil, albeit to a lesser extend than the [[The Istoria Rytsarey]]. His ultimate goal is to kill the Omnipotent One, **(add about his own sealing)** taking his place and commanding a self-built an army that would conquest the universe under absolute authority. To achieve this, he sought to harness the power of one or multiple objects (if he can), one being the orb (from Kindrack Diablo), another option being Jaloop's fragment of the Omnipotent One, and the final option book of narratives, all reality manipulating artifacts. He believes that these items can grant him divine and old one powers, elevating him above all mortals and ascending him to godhood. Arangol is highly condescending towards others; whenever confronted he is casual and uncaring, attempting to draw out conversations and make small talk. His pursuit of power is marked by intense pragmatism, as he sacrifices many lives to the Book of Narratives in attempt to unleash its full power. Despite his sadistic tendencies, Arangol doesn't kill indiscriminately as he typically warns others to not over step in attempt to stop him. 
Arangol's most defining trait is his arrogance which is outwardly shown as a god complex, that grows even further after merging with the **Book of Narratives** (He declares that no one besides him, not even the gods, has ever "stood upon the heavens" and believes himself superior to all in all aspects). He rarely shows any sign of alarm or distress, dismissing even the most powerful of opponents. However, Arangol's overconfidence and arrogance is his weakness, as it blinds him to failure. Whenever he is outmatched, he grows enraged when he realizes he is losing against a being he views as lesser. Arangol cannot comprehend how a mortal can match him showing his inability to recognize his own limitations, questioning why someone as brilliant as him would allow himself to be controlled by the Omnipotent One. This shows his belief in absolute independence and rejection of higher powers.
It is also hinted at that Arangol's obsession of his own power stems from a longing to be be recognized, as those emotionally intelligent enough can feel the loneliness seeping through his spells and martial combat. This brings the theory that the loss of power from the **Book of Narratives** was due to a hidden desire to be an ordinary person, born from the isolation of possessing extraordinary powers with out anyone able to understand his perspective, which the Book of Narratives gave him. 
Despite his arrogance, Arangol is still capable of showing respect towards others for their capabilities. He is rather humble when it comes to earning praise himself, often brushing off compliments about his praiseworthy actions. Arangol is also highly cautious, and often has entire situations planned out well in advance, as shown throughout the entire Felwinter Saga by his puppeteering control over events. However, per Arangol's own acknowledgment, he had become far less cautious after merging with the **Book of Narratives**
Arangol is highly manipulative and exploits the trust and emotions of those around him. He also displays barbaric cruelty and hypocrisy, taunting those in powerless positions and tells those who overpower him (pretty much no instances) to not get full of themselves for overpowering him in battle, despite his own arrogance. Arangol believes winners must dictate how the world should be, dismissing explanations of the role The Omnipotent One plays in the universe as the logic of a loser. Moreover, Arangol has a cynical view of mortal characteristics, seeing love and respect as the furthest state from understanding and trust as a cowardly reliance. To him, mortals depend on superiors out of blind faith, leading to the creation of kings and gods. Arangol believes only in harsh realities and denies the existence of truth and lies do not exist, believing that and that weak people cling to convenient untruths to avoid painful facts.
Arangol expresses particular faith in his forces, reassuring that they cannot be defeated as long as they stand together. He treats his subordinates as comrades and is polite, but is also harsh in disciplining. However, Arangol does not immediately punish all disobedience, and sometimes he instead shows understanding of certain actions. 
Ultimately though, Arangol views his subordinates as tools to be used and discarded at his convenience. Furthermore, he shows no mercy to those who betray him, and will ruthlessly cut them down or punish them for doing so. He has a willingness to exploit others is further exemplified by exploiting his forces and others under his command throughout the saga. Although he perceives his subordinates as disposable, he is still capable of expressing gratitude towards them, often thanking them for deeds that benefit himself. 
Arangol exhibits a deep prejudice against all mortals. He is visibly angered by the idea of being surpassed by a mere mortal and claims that **The Book of Narratives** cannot stand to see a one overpower him.

##### Brief History/Timeline   
###### Adolescence
Nothing is known about Arangol's upbringing
###### Felwinter Saga Cour 1
After Mephistopheles's defeat by Will Felwinter and his party and then Lady Pain's disappearance, Arangol saw a potential time to rise to power, with multiple major threats out of the picture. He followed Will's interdimensional travel to Sigil, where he established the Peacekeeper academy. Arangol saw an opportunity to seize power in the center of the mulitverse and then planned to expand it in order to create a universe that can defend itself from extra planar threats 
###### Twin Prodigies Arc
Arangol stayed within the shadows, and used his [[Necrowalkers]], [[Hostwalkers]], and other followers who he recruited or mind controlled to follow his cause to scout and slowly not only take over Sigil, but to also seek a young student with hihg potential who could serve as part of his conquest. He then found [[Azriel Darkstar]] and made his goal.
###### House Tournament Arc
At the end of the house tournament, Arangol made his move, invading Sigil along with a tiny part of his forces. In the fierce battle, Arangol showed great interest in Azriel, seeing his dark power, figuring he would be a perfect candidate to be his underling. He immediately targeted Azriel, though made sure to test him rather then actually fight him. Azriel was able to surprise him, making him sweat. Jackson then joins the fight with him, with the two engaging in a 2 versus 1. However, Arangol wanted to push him further, targeting Olivia to see how he would react. Azriel as predicted saved her from fatal harm and healed her. However she had no pulse and was unconscious, which casued Azriel to unleash his full dark power out of anger, and began to go head to head with Arangol. The Illithid Lich realized that if he didn't begin to try versus Jackson and Azriel he was gonna lose, so he began to use more of his power. Because of this, Jackson and Azriel were overwhelmed, with [[Will Felwinter]] making it just in time in order to stop and push back Arangol's forces. However Arangol left, he unleashed a dark power which was later discovered to be a power that was the predecessor to being able to use Abberant Runes. This killed Will Felwinter, and in addition he cast an unknown curse on Azriel. 
###### The Illithid Mage Arc
Azriel eventually sought Arangol for power and apprenticeship, to which Arangol agreed. He manipulated Azriel into thinking a world of absolute authority is the only way to protect those he loves. 
###### Betrayal Arc
Arangol ordered and oversaw Tartarus's (once Azriel now changed) crusade. He watched the conflict from afar. He congratulated Azriel on besting [[Jackson Felwinter]] and his old friends, and taking the runes that ascended him to a more powerful being. He then ordered Tartarus to prepare for a full scale war.
###### Declaration of War Arc
Arangol grew his forces and prepared for the large scale battle.
###### Panmnesia Arc
 > [!Warning]- Spoilers Ahead
 > Upon Tartarus's defeat, Arangol slinked back into the shadows and traveled to the Far Realm and temporarily a different universe. Because of this, he was unaffected by the Pannesia Spell. After the [[Peacekeeper Academy]] was shut down around 8 years after the events, it was transformed into a police and militaristic force dedicated to eradicating the evil that appears throughout the multiverse. During this time, Jackson's contingency plan kicked in, as the simulacrum that would appear if anything bad were to happen to him, began running the peacekeeper force due to him not returning from his mission. However, at some unknown time, an avatar of [[Arangol]] surprised and defeated the simulacrum, taking its place and running operations to take over different planes to gain power so he can kill the Omnipotent one and take his place. When this happened, Arangol manipulated the memories of almost all members of the [[Peacekeeper Force]] in order for no one to be suspicious. However, [[Aria Hassan]] knew the truth as Jackson before he left for his final task had shared the memories of the previous timeline with her. Arangol made sure to hire demons from the Abyss to capture Jackson, where they could do as they please with the Divine Champion Magus. Jackson was brought to the Abyss and Aria was given a bounty on her head as a framed traitor, which caused her to set out to save Jackson and run away from the force. Arangol had officially executed the first part of his plan successfully.
