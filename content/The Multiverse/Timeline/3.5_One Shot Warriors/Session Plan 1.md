**PREMISE (5–10 minutes)**

In the outer underground shadow of a small district within the city of Sigil within **Thespira**, a string of disappearances has drawn quiet panic. The victims share a pattern: vulnerable, marginalized, and forgotten by official channels. The district is attempting to quell the rising afford public fear. How do they do this? They hire adventurers, known as actors. Our party has been contracted by a mysterious masked intermediary known only as 

**Merchant of Vengeance** a middle man who has been contracted by a local law enforcement guild to find others that will do the dirty work for them, so that they can exterminate the threat while still keeping a righteous record. The job is simple on paper:

**Track down and eliminate violent offenders operating beyond the city’s walls.  No trials.  No mercy.  Proof required in any capacity.**

The payment is substantial, discretion will be mandatory. No greater evil may witness and survive. 

This is punishing work, so that hope for the victim can survive another night. 


**OPENING SCENE: FIRST LIGHT AT THE CAMP (10 minutes)**

**Location:** A broken road shrine overlooking a fog-choked ravine. A cold campfire smolders. The sun threatens the horizon but hasn’t risen yet.

- The party has arrived at the **last known location** tied to the suspects.
- A ruined **magitek train stop station** lies ahead, ruined by age and the ravine. 
- Locals won’t go near it. The Veil (magic) feels _wrong_ here.

Let the players settle, describe gear, scars, quiet rituals, or tension.

Then — “Before you step forward in adventurer… let’s remember how you got here.”


**FLASHBACK MONTAGE: THE WEEK’S JOURNEY (30 minutes total)**

**Structure:** ~5 minutes per player  
You prompt; they narrate. One roll each.  
Success = small boon. Failure = complication or scar (narrative, not punitive).

**Example Prompts by Class**


**ACT I: THE WAYSTATION (45–60 minutes)**

This is **not a dungeon**—it’s a **collapsed infrastructure ruin**, half natural, half magitek.

**Key Features**

- **Secret Descent:**  
	- A broken lift shaft leads downward. Climbing requires teamwork. Failure costs time or resources.
- **Environmental Trap:**  
	- Flickering Veil conduits surge unpredictably (DEX saves or minor damage / spell interference).
- **Intro Fight:**  
	- Heywire murderous warforged who attack the closest enemy, saying to stay away for their saftey 


**ACT II: THE LORE SCATTER (30–40 minutes)**
- Instead of one lore dump, the truth is **fragmented**.
- Each player finds a **different clue**, tailored to their strengths.

**Clues**
- Cell rooms 
	- Dented Walls
	- A letter never sent
	- A locket
	- A dead adventurer
- Makeshift medical Room 
	- Bodies being experimented on
	- Open carcasses
	- Altered medical Logs
	- A dead adventurer 
- Harem Room 
	- Chains and blood 
	- Bloodied Whips 
	- A dead rapist 
	- A dead adventurer
- Laboratory Room 
	- Strange liquids 
	- Strange fetus egg in a tank filled with strange liquid 
	- Blueprints for immortality
	- A dead adventurer
- Control Room
	- Broken power box 
	- Written camera logs 
	- A illusionary holographic communication system 
	- A dead adventurer 

What they _assume_ will shape the final confrontation.

  

**ACT III: THE CALL TO DUTY (10 minutes)**

They overhear a voice in the distance and have reached a point where there are recent traces of blood.

Give description of what the scene looks like and say its a point of no return
  

**FINAL ENCOUNTER: THE BROKEN STAGE (30–45 minutes)**

**Arena:** A collapsed amphitheater-like chamber once used for public transit announcements.  
Now repurposed.

**Enemies**
- A evil necromancer
- A few zombie minions 

**Mechanic**
- 6 pillars, each gives the evil necromancer a certain ability or buff, and also changes the stage 
	- Red pillar, gives the necromancer increased damage +
	- Orange pillar, gives the necromancer 2 actions
	- Yellow pillar, gives the necromancer increased spell damage +
	- Green, gives the necromancer regeneration 
	- Blue, gives the necromancer a +2 to their AC and saving throws  
	- Purple, gives the necromancer special mmo actions
	- Black and white, gives the necromancer immortality 



Let player assumptions matter:

- If they thought this was ritualistic → magic backlash
- If they thought it was mercantile → escape attempt
- If they thought it was ideological → final monologue

  

**RESOLUTION (10 minutes)**

When the dust settles:

- Survivors (if any) try to bargain, justify, or flee.
- Proof is collected.
- The water flow begins to cleanse.

They are paid... Quietly.

  

**HOOK FOR FUTURE SESSIONS**

A tattooed crest across his neck. Someone funded this. Someone protected them. And this was not an isolated act.
_“The audience prefers silence.”_
  

**ENDING NOTE TO PLAYERS**

You didn’t save the world tonight.

You made sure it didn’t get worse.

And in **The Grand Requiem** that is how legends begin.
