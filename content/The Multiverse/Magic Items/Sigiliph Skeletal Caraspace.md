Sigiliph Skeletal Caraspace is [[Emily Sigiliph]] and [[Johnny Sigiliph]]'s human skeleton
###### Abilities 
The Skeletal Caraspace is the bone structure of the Sigiliph siblings. 
- The Skeleton cannot be removed against your will
- The Skeleton Functions as adamantine armor 
- The Skeleton Functions as a Blood Fury Tattoo. It has a a number of charges equal to your proficiency bonus and regains all of them after 24 hours
	- When you hit a creature with a weapon attack, you can expend a charge to deal an extra 4d6 necrotic damage to the target, and you regain a number of hit points equal to the necrotic damage dealt.
	- When a creature you can see damages you, you can expend a charge and use your reaction to make a melee attack against that creature, with advantage on your attack roll.
- Your movement speed increases by 5 and your strength is set to 22
- Awakened Power 
	- Once per day for 5 minutes, A Sigiliph can use their full power from their rigorous training from the secret training regiment that they preformed. They gains 50 temporary hp, advantage on all weapon attack rolls, deals extra 2d12 force damage, crits on a 19-20 and doubles and gives a flying speed until the end of their turns. This can be deactivated as a bonus action to conserve time. 
		- **If she is not wearing a restrictor**
			- The Sigiliph can use their full physical ability. All of their physical stats are raised to 30, removes the time limit on awakened power and it removes their fundamental barrier that restricts their physical growth. However, while in this state they become less competent in other areas and can be outperformed by others will skills in areas outside of fighting, for example like Rock Paper Scissors or Board games, mostly in one-sided matches. 
				- **Notes:** The player who possess this ability has to work with the DM's discretion and reason, in 99% of cases causes cinematic scenes. The character is still beatable though, in whatever way makes sense for the DM
